Chicken and rice is one of the most useful dinner combinations because it can go in almost any direction.

The problem is that "chicken and rice" sounds plain until you choose a format. Once you pick the format, dinner gets much easier.

## Start with the format

Here are five reliable options:

- **Bowl:** chicken, rice, vegetables, sauce, something crunchy
- **Skillet:** chicken, rice, broth, vegetables, seasoning
- **Soup:** chicken, rice, carrots, celery, onion, broth
- **Fried rice:** leftover rice, chicken, eggs, frozen vegetables, soy sauce
- **Burrito bowl:** chicken, rice, beans, salsa, cheese, lettuce

## What to add

Chicken and rice needs contrast. Add one item from each group:

- **Fresh:** cucumber, greens, cilantro, lime, tomato, cabbage
- **Creamy:** yogurt sauce, avocado, cheese, tahini, sour cream
- **Crunchy:** toasted nuts, tortilla chips, sesame seeds, crisp lettuce
- **Saucy:** teriyaki, salsa, curry sauce, pesto, hot honey, vinaigrette

You do not need all of them. One fresh thing and one sauce can make the meal feel done.

## If the chicken is already cooked

Do not recook it hard. Add it near the end so it warms without drying out.

Good formats for cooked chicken:

- Chicken fried rice
- Chicken taco bowls
- Chicken soup
- Chicken Caesar rice bowls
- Chicken pesto rice bake

Cooked chicken is also ideal for next-day lunch. Turn it into wraps, bowls, or soup before it becomes a mystery container.

## If the rice is leftover

Leftover rice is better for fried rice than fresh rice. It is drier, so it crisps and separates.

Use high heat, a little oil, and do not crowd the pan. Add vegetables first, then rice, then chicken, then egg or sauce.

## The simple dinner answer

When in doubt, make a bowl:

1. Warm the rice.
2. Slice or shred the chicken.
3. Add vegetables.
4. Add sauce.
5. Finish with something fresh or crunchy.

That is dinner. It does not need to be more complicated than that.
